URBAN OPTIMA

ID: 23FE10CSE00226

AI-Driven Cross-Domain Smart City Optimization Platform

Problem Statement

Urban infrastructures operate in isolated silos. Waste systems, traffic monitoring, air quality analysis, and energy systems lack unified intelligence. This results in inefficient decision-making, increased environmental impact, and poor resource allocation. There is a need for an AI-based centralized platform capable of performing cross-domain analytics for intelligent urban governance.

Literature Review

Existing smart city systems focus on domain-specific solutions such as traffic optimization or pollution monitoring. While IoT integration and AI-based systems exist, multi-domain integration with scalable modular architecture remains limited.

Research Gap & Innovation

Urban Optima introduces cross-domain predictive analytics by integrating heterogeneous urban datasets. The platform correlates waste density, traffic congestion, and environmental metrics to enable predictive decision-making rather than isolated forecasting.

System Architecture

Frontend (React Dashboard) → Flask REST APIs → AI Processing Layer (YOLOv8 + Regression Models) → Data Aggregation Layer → Visualization & Decision Support.


The architecture is modular, scalable, and supports integration of additional smart city domains.

Cross-Domain Predictive Analysis

Urban Optima leverages multi-source urban datasets to perform predictive modeling across interconnected subsystems. For example, traffic congestion patterns can influence pollution levels, which correlate with waste accumulation in commercial zones. By applying multivariate regression techniques, the system improves forecasting reliability across domains.

Technology Stack

Frontend: React + Vite
Backend: Flask REST APIs
AI Framework: PyTorch, YOLOv8
Analytics: Regression Models
Visualization: Chart.js
Deployment: GitHub Pages

Comparative Analysis

Compared to traditional single-domain systems, Urban Optima demonstrates improved accuracy, predictive reliability, and integration efficiency through centralized analytics and AI-driven architecture.
